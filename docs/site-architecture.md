# Oddity Store Site Architecture

## 1. Core Structure

The website should balance three things:

- homepage sells the brand through products
- collection pages organize the product universe
- shop pages hold the full catalog

Recommended top navigation:

- Keycaps
- Keyboards
- Desk Mats
- Cups
- Cases
- About

The logo should act as the `Home` link, so `Home` does not need to occupy a separate navigation slot.

Utility icons on the right side of the header:

- Search
- Account / Login
- Cart

`Shipping & FAQ` should not stay in the primary navigation.
It should live in the footer, product pages, and service-info sections where users actually need it.

Optional later:

- Archive
- Journal

### Category-first navigation strategy

Instead of putting one large `Products` item in the navigation, the product categories themselves can become the main navigation.

Recommended first-level product categories:

- Keycaps
- Keyboards
- Desk Mats
- Cups
- Cases

This keeps the header direct and commercial, while avoiding an oversized mega-menu.

Support links can be placed elsewhere:

- `Shop All` inside the search overlay, homepage section, or category pages
- `Collections` as a homepage entry block and secondary page link
- `New Releases` as a homepage module or small highlighted link near the hero
- `Coming Soon` inside product listing filters or category tabs

## 2. Homepage Structure

### Section 1: Hero Carousel

Large rotating visual banners.
Each slide should highlight a product or collection.

Recommended content per slide:

- large product image
- short title
- one-line concept copy
- CTA: `Shop Now`
- CTA: `View Collection`

Recommended launch with 3 to 5 slides:

- Literary hero product
- Magic hero product
- Great Writer keyboard
- Cup or desk mat story

The hero should include optional support text for the brand, but the visual center must stay on product.

### Section 2: New Releases

A product grid for the latest or most important SKUs.

Purpose:

- make the homepage immediately feel commercial
- show that the site is active

Recommended count:

- 4 to 8 products

### Section 3: Shop By Category

This section supports the first-level category navigation and gives fast access to all merchandise types.

Recommended cards:

- Keycaps
- Keyboards
- Desk Mats
- Cups
- Cases

### Section 4: Shop By Collection

Visual entry cards for major groups:

- Literary Archives
- Arcane Studies
- Entry Points
- Great Writer Boards
- Desk Mats
- Cup Series

### Section 5: Featured Products

This is a curated middle layer between homepage and full catalog.

Recommended content:

- bestselling keycap sets
- one signature Great Writer keyboard
- one hero cup product

### Section 6: Reviews & Community Proof

This section should make the site feel trusted and alive.

Recommended content types:

- customer testimonials
- short buyer quotes
- photos or review snapshots
- creator or blogger highlights

Possible content labels:

- What collectors are saying
- Seen on creators
- Community field notes

### Section 7: Media / Video Showcase

This section should pull in content from your own channels and external creator coverage.

Recommended formats:

- video thumbnails linking out to YouTube / Instagram / TikTok
- creator cards with quote plus preview image
- studio content cards showing new launches, packaging, or setup shots

The goal is not to dump social links, but to prove the products exist in the real world and are being shown by people.

### Section 8: What We Offer

This section is the equivalent of the confidence-building service area found on stronger commerce sites.

Recommended content:

- design by our studio
- curated themed collections
- worldwide shipping
- release support
- responsive customer service
- collectible packaging or premium presentation

This section should answer: why buy from Oddity Store instead of a generic seller?

### Section 9: Brand Story

Shorter than a typical fashion-brand story page.
The purpose is to explain why the products feel distinct.

Suggested topics:

- story-driven design
- archival and found-document aesthetic
- collectible packaging and visual language

### Section 10: Social Presence

Give your own brand channels a dedicated place.

Recommended links:

- Instagram
- YouTube
- TikTok
- other active channels as needed

This section can also include:

- follow us for drops
- behind the scenes
- launch reminders

### Section 11: International Shopping Info

Short cards:

- worldwide shipping
- support response
- release type: in stock / pre-order / limited drop
- payment confidence

### Section 12: Footer

Include:

- contact
- Instagram / social links
- shipping policy
- FAQ
- copyright and brand line

## 3. Shop / Products Structure

Even with category-first navigation, the site should still have a complete catalog structure.

This can exist as:

- a hidden `Shop All` landing page
- a searchable overlay
- or a discoverable page linked from homepage modules and footer

The full catalog layer should include:

- all active products
- filters
- sort
- product status

Recommended filters:

- category
- collection
- availability
- price range

Recommended category structure:

- `Keycaps / 键帽`
- includes keycap sets and keycap protective films
- `Keyboards / 键盘`
- includes your Great Writer keyboard products, keyboard kits, and laptop keyboard protectors
- `Desk Mats / 桌垫`
- `Cups / 杯子`
- `Cases / 壳类`
- includes phone cases and earphone cases

Recommended product statuses:

- In Stock
- Pre-Order
- Coming Soon
- Sold Out

## 4. Community Content

Community does not need to be a top-level navigation item in V1.
It works better as a strong homepage section and can later become a dedicated page if the content volume grows.

## 5. Services Content

Services also does not need to be a top-level navigation item in V1.
It should first appear as a homepage trust-building section and later become a standalone page only if necessary.

## 6. Community Page

This page is for trust and social proof.

Recommended blocks:

- customer review wall
- creator spotlight
- embedded or linked video content
- press or blog mentions
- gallery of user setups

## 7. Services Page

This page explains what the studio provides beyond listing products.

Recommended blocks:

- in-house design direction
- themed product development
- release planning
- support and shipping help
- collaborations or custom opportunities if offered later

## 8. Collection System

Collections should work as both brand storytelling and product organization.

Recommended initial collections:

- Literary Archives
- Arcane Studies
- Entry Points
- Great Writer Boards
- Desk Mats
- Cup Series
- Cases

Each collection page should include:

- short intro
- hero image
- collection product grid
- optional archive strip of older related products

## 9. Product Page Template

Every product page should support international conversion.

Recommended blocks:

- large gallery
- product name
- short concept line
- price
- status
- buy button
- shipping note
- product details
- inspiration / design notes
- included keys or specifications
- related products

## 10. Catalog Strategy

The site can hold all products, but not all products need equal homepage priority.

Recommended hierarchy:

- Homepage: only flagship and latest products
- Collection pages: all products by world or theme
- Shop All: complete catalog
- Later Archive: sold-out and older drops

This keeps the site premium without hiding your range.

## 11. Homepage Content Sources

To support the new homepage sections, we will eventually need these content pools:

- hero product images
- product thumbnails for featured and new releases
- customer quotes or review screenshots
- creator / blogger posts or video links
- your own social account links
- short service descriptions

If some of these are missing at the beginning, the design can launch with placeholders and be upgraded later.

## 12. Risk-Sensitive Naming Layer

For products with potential IP exposure:

- keep internal product meaning
- use safer public collection labels
- revise obvious franchise-forward titles if needed
- avoid risky wording in hero banners and top-level navigation

This is a naming and copy problem more than a design problem.

## 13. Recommended V1 Build Order

1. Homepage
2. Products / Shop All page
3. Collection page template
4. Product page template
5. Community + Services
6. About + Shipping FAQ

## 14. Immediate Next Decision

Before visual implementation, finalize:

- top navigation names
- homepage section order
- first 3 to 5 hero carousel subjects
- first 8 featured products
